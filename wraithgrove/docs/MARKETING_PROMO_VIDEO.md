## §1 — Video Specs

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| Total duration | 15 seconds |
| Primary format | 9:16 vertical (App Store preview, TikTok, Instagram Reels) |
| Secondary format | 16:9 landscape (Play Store promo video, YouTube) |
| Resolution | 1080×1920 (9:16) / 1920×1080 (16:9) |
| Frame rate | 30fps minimum; 60fps preferred for combat sequences |

## §2 — Creative Brief

**Emotional arc in 15 seconds:** Safe → Wrong → Thrilling → Vast → Terrifying → Yours

**Structural logic:**
- Shots 1-2: Set the hook. The player thinks they're in a calm game. Then something is wrong.
- Shots 3-4: Escalation. Combat is real. The system is deep.
- Shot 5: The boss. One image that makes someone watch again.
- Shot 6: CTA. The game's identity in a frame, plus the ask.

**The viewer:** A mobile gamer who played Vampire Survivors and wants something with more atmosphere. They've seen a hundred "epic RPG" trailers. They will skip in 2 seconds unless the first frame is strange.

**The hook:** The first frame is calm on purpose. The rug-pull is the hook.

## §3 — Six-Shot Storyboard

### Shot 1 — THE WELCOME (0:00–0:02)
**Duration:** 2 seconds
**Description:** Stage 1 opening. Player character walks slowly down a lantern-lit stone path. Cherry blossoms drift sideways in a gentle wind. The scene is beautiful and quiet — this is not the game most people expect.
**Camera:** Slow track — dolly forward behind the character at walking pace. Slight handheld sway, 2px amplitude. Camera is low, character fills bottom 40% of frame.
**Action:** Character walks. Blossoms drift. One lantern on the left post flickers very briefly (1 frame) — the first lie.
**On-screen text:** None. No title card. No genre label. Let the calm breathe.
**Audio cue:** Gentle shamisen, single repeating melody. Distant temple bell strikes once at 1:50. Fades into...

---

### Shot 2 — THE TURN (0:02–0:04)
**Duration:** 2 seconds
**Camera:** Same dolly — then the character stops. Camera continues forward half a step and settles. We're closer than we were.
**Action:** The lanterns ahead flicker out, left to right, one after another — a wave of darkness rolling toward the player. A wraith silhouette retreats backward into the treeline at the edge of frame. It was standing on the path. It was watching. Now it's gone.
**On-screen text:** `The lanterns lie.` — centered, brush-stroke white, appears at 0:03 and holds 1 second
**Audio cue:** The shamisen hits a dissonant chord — not jarring, just wrong. The temple bell does not strike again when you expect it to.

---

### Shot 3 — COMBAT PEAK (0:04–0:07)
**Duration:** 3 seconds
**Camera:** Hard cut from the quiet path to mid-combat. Stage 8. Camera pulls back fast (0.5s), then stabilizes — we see the full arena.
**Action:** Player character launches a signature ability — ink-wash energy wave expanding outward. Three wraiths are hit, shatter into particle flurries. Enemies respawn at frame edges. The HUD is minimal — one health bar, one cooldown indicator. The player's movement leaves ink-streak trails.
**Sub-beat:** At 0:06, briefly flash to Fever Mode — screen vignette turns deep crimson, character glows, the hit-stop effect intensifies. 15-frame burst only. Just enough to say: there are higher states of play.
**On-screen text:** `18 stages.` — appears at 0:05, bottom-third placement, 40% opacity white
**Audio cue:** Sharp percussion hit on the signature ability release. The shamisen returns — faster, harder, with added bass. Crowd-of-whispers building underneath.

---

### Shot 4 — TOWER GAUNTLET / SYSTEM DEPTH (0:07–0:09)
**Duration:** 2 seconds
**Camera:** Cut to Tower Gauntlet UI. Not gameplay — the floor counter and buff card pick screen. This is the "content is vast" beat.
**Action:** Floor counter spins upward — Floor 12 → 27 → 51. Speed accelerates. Three buff cards flip face-up in rapid succession: PHANTOM STEP / BONE WARD / LANTERN SHATTER. Each card has a ukiyo-e illustration.
**On-screen text:** `Endless Tower Gauntlet.` — top-left, small, appears with the first floor number
**Audio cue:** Drum roll tight and fast, building under the spinning counter. Whispers escalate, multiply, almost recognizable as words but not quite.

---

### Shot 5 — BOSS REVEAL (0:09–0:13)
**Duration:** 4 seconds
**Camera:** Hard cut to a full-frame slow reveal. The camera starts at ground level — we see only the Wraith Father's feet, then tilts up over 2 seconds to reveal the full silhouette. Hold on the full reveal for 1.5 seconds.
**Action:** The Wraith Father rises behind a torii gate at the far end of a stone courtyard. Horned. Massive. The player character is visible in the lower foreground, tiny, lantern raised — one arm extended, the lantern at full extension toward the creature.
**Key detail:** The Wraith Father's single red iris focuses on the lantern. Not on the player. On the light.
**On-screen text:** None during the reveal. After the full hold: `Six bosses await.` — small, centered, at 0:12. Hold 0.5s.
**Audio cue:** Complete silence at the moment the full silhouette appears — 0.75 seconds of total silence. Then one long deep horn, held until the cut. The whispers stop entirely.

---

### Shot 6 — CALL TO ACTION (0:13–0:15)
**Duration:** 2 seconds
**Camera:** Graphic cut — not gameplay footage. Title card treatment.
**Action:** Black screen. The "Unlimited Chaos" title renders in brush-stroke calligraphy, ink bleeding across the screen from left to right as if being painted in real time. Below it: `Folk Horror Survival Arena` in small clean caps. Below that: the App Store / Play Store badge.
**On-screen text (the tagline):** `Don't trust the lanterns.` appears last, smallest, in italic — 0.75 seconds in. Holds to end.
**Audio cue:** Final shamisen note — one clean pluck, held. Fades to silence. No CTA voice-over.

## §4 — Voice-Over Script

**Word count rule:** Under 30 words total.

### Primary VO (with narrator)
> "The grove welcomes you. It lied. Hunt wraiths through 18 stages of folk-horror arena survival. Trust no lantern. Unlimited Chaos — out now."

**Word count:** 28 words. ✓

**Placement:** VO begins at Shot 2 (0:02). Pacing: one breath per shot. Silent during Shot 5 (boss reveal) — the silence is mandatory. Resume VO at Shot 6 CTA.

**Voice direction:** Male or female, low register. Calm, almost neutral — not dramatic. The calmness against the visuals creates the dread. Do not cast someone who sounds like a game trailer narrator. Cast someone who sounds like they are recounting something that happened to them.

### Subtitle track (mandatory for muted autoplay)
Every VO line must have an on-screen subtitle displayed in clean white on a semi-transparent bar. Position at bottom 15% of frame. Font size: legible at 1/3 screen width.

## §5 — Fallback: Music-Only Non-VO Variant

**Use case:** App Store preview (VO is often stripped for international markets), social ads in sound-sensitive contexts, YouTube pre-roll.

**Replace VO with:**
- Expand on-screen text cards to carry the narrative content:
  - Shot 2 text: `Something is wrong.` (replaces the VO "The grove welcomed you. It lied.")
  - Shot 3 text: `Hunt 18 stages.` (replaces genre/count VO)
  - Shot 5 text: `Six bosses.` stays as-is
  - Shot 6 text: `Unlimited Chaos` / `Folk Horror Survival Arena` / `Don't trust the lanterns.` / `Download now.`

**Music:** Same track as primary version. The shamisen score carries the arc without VO — the dissonant chord at Shot 2 and the silence at Shot 5 are load-bearing emotional beats even without a narrator.

**Note for video worker:** The music-only variant requires only re-cut of text cards — it is not a separate edit. Edit the primary version, then swap text cards in a second pass and export as Variant 2.

## §6 — Production Notes

**Who this is for:** A mobile gamer who will see this as an App Store preview thumbnail or a social ad. They are scrolling fast. The first frame (the calm path) is the hook — it is intentionally not the most exciting frame. If we open with combat, we look like every other game. If we open with silence and then break it, we stand apart.

**Duration note:** 15 seconds is tight. Do not add a 5-second intro card with the studio logo. The Architect is pre-launch with no brand recognition to pay off — spend every second on the game's identity, not the studio's.

**Fever Mode note:** Shot 3 references Fever Mode (W-Fever-Mode.done). Confirm with orchestrator that Fever Mode is in the shippable build before committing to the visual in the promo. If it's not ship-ready, cut the 15-frame burst from Shot 3 — the shot works without it.

**Aspect ratio for submission:**
- Apple App Store: 9:16 vertical (mandatory for previews)
- Google Play: 16:9 landscape (mandatory; Play Store does not accept 9:16 as the primary promo video)
- Produce both from the same edit — shoot gameplay in 16:9, reframe to 9:16 with safe-zone masking
