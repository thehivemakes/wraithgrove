## §1 — Canvas Specs

### 1.1 Google Play Store Feature Graphic
- **Dimensions:** 1024 × 500 px
- **Safe zone:** Keep all text and focal elements inside an 896 × 416 px centered rectangle (44px margin each side). Play Console crops unpredictably on older devices.
- **Format:** PNG or JPEG, max 1 MB
- **Orientation:** Landscape only
- **Color space:** sRGB
- **No text requirement note:** Play Store feature graphic is displayed above the app listing in search results — the game title is visible in the listing itself, so text overlay is optional but recommended for brand recall.

### 1.2 Apple App Store Wide Hero (App Preview header / promotional banner)
- **Dimensions:** 4320 × 1080 px (4:1 ratio, ultra-wide)
- **Safe zone:** Keep primary focal elements inside 3840 × 900 px (240px margin sides, 90px top/bottom). Apple crops this banner at multiple breakpoints across device sizes; the center-left 1/3 of the image is the most reliable visible zone on small devices.
- **Format:** PNG, max 3 MB
- **Color space:** sRGB
- **Usage:** App Preview header on App Store product page (above screenshot carousel). Also doubles as hero asset for social/press.
- **Text requirement:** Include game title in the safe zone at minimum. Subtitle and tagline are optional given cramped dimensions — prioritize art over copy here.

## §2 — Three Layout Variants

All three variants share:
- **Brand register:** Eastern folk-horror, ukiyo-e ink-wash, no modern UI chrome
- **Mandatory element:** The single lit lantern as focal point — the only warm color in the frame
- **Typography:** Brush-stroke calligraphic font for "Unlimited Chaos" title; clean sans-serif for subtitle/tagline at small sizes
- **Tone:** Dread without gore. The image must feel like standing on a dark road holding a candle.

---

### Variant A — Boss vs. Player (RECOMMENDED for Play Store)

**Concept:** One player. One lantern. One enormous monster. The full Unlimited Chaos pitch in a single composition.

**Composition:**
- Upper-right two-thirds of frame: the Wraith Father silhouette looming — horned, massive, partially veiled in ink-wash storm cloud
- Lower-left third: a small player character, seen from behind, holding a single lit lantern at arm's length
- The lantern is the brightest pixel in the entire image — a concentrated point of orange-amber warmth
- Torii gate silhouette in the mid-ground at the horizon, half-obscured by fog — frames the confrontation as ritual, not combat
- Wraith Father detail: one arterial-red iris visible in the silhouette, the only color on the creature

**Text overlay:**
- `UNLIMITED CHAOS` in ukiyo-e brush-stroke font, centered at the horizon line between player and creature
- Below: `Folk Horror Survival Arena` in clean small caps, 60% opacity white
- Bottom-right corner: tagline `Don't trust the lanterns.` in italics, 40% opacity

**Color priority:**
1. The lantern (orange-amber #E8740A, hard circle of light, diameter ~8% of canvas width)
2. The fog (silver-blue #B8C8D4, mid-ground, softens the boundary between player and wraith)
3. The wraith (ink-black #0D0D0D to #1A1A2E gradient)
4. Ground (cold blue-grey #2A2D3E, ink texture visible)
5. Background (near-black #050508, slight blue-purple undertone)

**Emotional sentence this image must communicate:** "You have a tiny light against an enormous dark, and the light is what's drawing the dark to you."

### Variant B — Hero Character Center

**Concept:** The protagonist front and center, the grove framing them. Character-RPG first-impression.

**Composition:**
- Center frame: one walker character in full ukiyo-e portrait stance — not attacking, just standing, looking slightly off-axis (not directly at viewer)
- Character holds the lit lantern at chest height; their face is half-lit, half-shadow
- Left side: deep forest of bare ink-wash trees receding into fog, subtle wraith silhouettes barely visible at treeline
- Right side: mirrored forest, but one tree has luminous red eyes embedded in the bark
- Above: overcast sky with visible moon through storm clouds, moonlight competing with the lantern
- Below character: stone path with a fallen torii gate partially blocking the path

**Text overlay:**
- `UNLIMITED CHAOS` large, brush-stroke, above character's head — centered, 95% opacity
- `Folk Horror Survival Arena` subtitle below title, smaller, 80% opacity
- No tagline — this variant is character-focus, not mood-focus

**Focal hierarchy:** Character face → lantern flame → tree eyes → title text

**Composition note for Play Store (1024×500):** At 1024×500 the character will fill ~40% of the vertical height. The trees must be cropped aggressively — keep the eye-bearing tree fully in frame (right side). For App Store wide hero (4320×1080) the character is a narrow vertical in the center third; the forest extends full-width on both sides, which emphasizes isolation.

---

### Variant C — 4-Character Lineup

**Concept:** Four walkers side by side, each in a distinct biome lighting. Signals roster depth and character-RPG investment.

**Composition:**
- Four character panels, equal width, seamed with ink-wash brushstroke dividers (not hard borders)
- Left to right: walker in summer forest (warm gold-green light), walker in cold stone biome (blue lantern light), walker in lantern temple (deep red altar light), walker in Eldritch tier (no clear light source — their own eyes faintly glow)
- Each character holds something different: lantern / ritual knife / prayer beads / nothing (bare hands raised)
- All four look toward the same off-screen point — convergent gaze creates implicit shared threat

**Text overlay:**
- `UNLIMITED CHAOS` spanning full width, brush-stroke font, positioned at top 15% of frame
- `Nine walkers. Each broken differently.` as a centered caption at bottom 10%
- Each character panel captioned with a one-word trait at portrait base: GUARDIAN / EXILE / PENITENT / UNBOUND (use 3 unlocked + 1 silhouetted locked to tease roster depth)

**Use case:** This variant is strongest on App Store wide hero (4320×1080) where horizontal real estate shows all four characters. It compresses poorly at Play Store 1024×500 — only use Variant C there if A/B testing suggests character-roster hook outperforms boss-threat hook for your specific audience. Default to Variant A for Play Store.

**Focal hierarchy:** Eldritch walker (rightmost, most striking) → summer forest walker (leftmost, most welcoming) → shared gaze direction → title

## §3 — Color Palette

| Role | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| Lantern core | `#E8740A` | The single hot point. Appears in every variant. Never diluted. |
| Lantern halo | `#F4A93C` | 2–4px soft bloom around lantern core |
| Ink black | `#0D0D0D` | Wraith silhouette, deep shadow fills |
| Void blue | `#1A1A2E` | Background gradient in extreme shadows |
| Ink grey | `#2A2D3E` | Ground texture, stone surfaces |
| Fog silver | `#B8C8D4` | Mid-ground atmosphere, the boundary between known and unknown |
| Cold moonlight | `#D0DCEB` | Ambient sky, bare tree highlights |
| Arterial red | `#8B0000` | Wraith iris only — single use, maximum impact |
| Ukiyo-e parchment | `#F2E8D5` | Title text fill when rendering on dark background |
| Bone white | `#E8E0D0` | Character clothing highlights, dried wood |

**Palette rule:** The lantern (#E8740A) should be the highest-saturation element in the entire image. Everything else desaturates to serve it. The moment a secondary element competes with the lantern for warmth, the composition's emotional logic breaks.

## §4 — Typography Hooks

### Game Title — "UNLIMITED CHAOS"
- **Font style:** Ukiyo-e brush-stroke / Japanese woodblock print aesthetic. Thick horizontal strokes, thin vertical, slight ink-bleed effect at stroke ends.
- **Approximate Western analog:** *Cinzel Decorative* (bold) with ink-texture overlay is a fallback if custom brush font unavailable. *Trajan Pro* is the absolute minimum fallback — do not use modern sans.
- **Size:** Title should occupy no less than 15% and no more than 25% of canvas height.
- **Color:** Ukiyo-e parchment #F2E8D5 fill with 2px near-black (#0D0D0D) stroke — legible on both dark and mid-tone backgrounds.
- **Effect:** Subtle ink-bleed on stroke terminals. No drop shadow — drop shadow is a modern UI artifact that breaks the art-piece illusion.

### Subtitle — "Folk Horror Survival Arena" / "Veil of Lanterns"
- **Font style:** Clean small-caps, low contrast, slightly tracked out (+100 letter-spacing)
- **Approximate Western analog:** *Cormorant Garamond* small caps, or similar old-style serif
- **Size:** 40–50% of title size
- **Color:** Fog silver #B8C8D4 at 80% opacity
- **Placement:** Directly below title, ~20% of title's line-height spacing

### Tagline — "Don't trust the lanterns."
- **Font style:** Italic, light weight, slightly smaller than subtitle
- **Color:** Same parchment at 50% opacity, or cold moonlight #D0DCEB
- **Placement:** Bottom-right corner, not centered — feels like a whisper, not a headline

## §6 — Art Worker Handoff Notes

1. **Variant A is the primary.** Build A1 (Play Store) first, verify composition logic, then scale to A2 (App Store).
2. **Test the lantern rule.** After generation, convert image to greyscale. In greyscale, the lantern area should still be the lightest point in the frame. If it isn't, the color balance is wrong.
3. **The wraith must not look like a game character.** It is a shape, a silhouette, a presence. No armor, no clear anatomy. Only the single red iris as a detail.
4. **No UI elements.** No health bars, no currency counters, no HUD chrome. The featured graphic must look like a piece of art, not a screenshot.
5. **Deliver at 2× native resolution** before final crop/export to maintain sharpness on retina displays.
6. **MJ iteration note:** If the first MJ output places the figure too centrally (MJ defaults to centered subjects), add `--no centered composition, --no symmetry` and re-run.
